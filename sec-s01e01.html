    Section 1.1 Episode 1: Addition and Subtraction

    Episode 1 Learning Goals.

    Episode 1 Key Symbols.

    \begin{equation*} + \hspace{1.0in}\text{as in}\,\, 3 + 2 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} - \hspace{1.0in}\text{as in}\,\, 4 - 1 \end{equation*}

    Definition 1.1.1.

    Addition is the operation that corresponds to combining piles of similar objects. Addition is represented using the symbol \(+\) (“plus”).
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.1.2.

    What is \(4+2\text{?}\)
    🔗
    Solution: Create a pile containing 4 objects. Create a second pile containing 2 objects. Put those piles together and count the number of objects you have in total.
    🔗
    🟢🟢🟢🟢 + 🟢🟢 = 🟢🟢🟢🟢🟢🟢
    🔗
    In total, we have 6 objects, so: \(4+2=6\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.1.3.

    If you have 3 slices of leftover pepperoni pizza and 2 slices of leftover cheese pizza, how many slices of pizza do you have in total?
    🔗
    Solution: Since we’re putting together two piles of pizza slices, this is an addition problem. Specifically, this is the problem
    \begin{equation*} 3+2 \end{equation*}
    We can answer the problem by counting: 🍕🍕🍕 + 🍕🍕=🍕🍕🍕🍕🍕
    🔗
    In other words, we have a total of \(3+2 = 5\) slices of pizza.
    🔗
    🔗

    Notice Something Neat.

    You can change the order of the numbers in an addition problem and still get the same answer!
    🔗
    \(4+1=5 \) because 🟢🟢🟢🟢 + 🟢 = 🟢🟢🟢🟢🟢
    🔗
    \(1+4=5 \) because 🟢 + 🟢🟢🟢🟢 = 🟢🟢🟢🟢🟢
    🔗
    🔗
    For problems involving larger numbers, you can use the <+> button on your calculator to perform addition.
    🔗

    Example 1.1.4.

    You have $214 in your bank account, and then you deposit another $1371. Now how much money do you have in your account?
    🔗
    Solution: Since you’re combining two piles of money, this is an addition problem, specifically the problem
    \begin{equation*} 214+1371 \end{equation*}
    We can use the \(+\) button on our calculator to find the answer to this problem:
    \begin{equation*} 214+1371=1585 \end{equation*}
    So that means we have $1585 in the bank account now.
    🔗
    🔗
    There are a couple of common key words that indicate that we need to perform addition. These include:
    • Total
      🔗
      🔗
    • Combine
      🔗
      🔗
    • Sum
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.1.5.

    What is the sum of 8 and 3?
    🔗
    Solution: This is asking us to add 8 and 3.
    \begin{equation*} 8+3 = 11 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 1.1.6.

    Subtraction is the operation that corresponds to removing objects from a pile. Subtraction is represented using the symbol \(-\) (“minus” or “take away”).
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.1.7.

    What is \(5-2\text{?}\)
    🔗
    Solution: Create a pile containing 5 objects. Remove 2 objects from that pile. Then count the number of objects you have left.
    🔗
    🟢🟢🟢🟢🟢 - 🟢🟢 = 🟢🟢🟢
    🔗
    3 objects remain in the pile, so: \(5-2=3\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.1.8.

    If you have 8 eggs and use 3 of them in a recipe, how many eggs do you have left?
    🔗
    Solution: Since we’re removing eggs from a pile, this is a subtraction problem. \(8-3\) We can answer the problem by counting:
    🔗
    🥚🥚🥚🥚🥚🥚🥚🥚 - 🥚🥚🥚 = 🥚🥚🥚🥚🥚
    🔗
    In other words, we have \(8-3=5\) remaining eggs.
    🔗
    🔗
    For problems involving larger numbers, you can use the <-> button on your calculator to perform addition.
    🔗

    Example 1.1.9.

    You have $523 in your bank account, and then you spend $138 on groceries. Now how much money do you have in your account?
    🔗
    Solution: Since you’re taking away money for a pile of money, this is a subtraction problem, specifically the problem
    \begin{equation*} 523 - 138 \end{equation*}
    We can use the + button on our calculator to find the answer to this problem:
    \begin{equation*} 523 - 138 = 385 \end{equation*}
    So that means we have $385 in the bank account now.
    🔗
    🔗
    There are a couple of common key words that indicate that we need to perform subtraction. These include:
    • Remove
      🔗
      🔗
    • Take Away
      🔗
      🔗
    • Decrease
      🔗
      🔗
    • Difference
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.1.10.

    What is the difference between 10 and 4?
    🔗
    Solution: This is asking us to subtract 10 and 4.
    \begin{equation*} 10-4=6. \end{equation*}
    🔗
