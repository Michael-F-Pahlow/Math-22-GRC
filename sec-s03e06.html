    • Ratio
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Episode 6 Key Symbols.

    \begin{equation*} : \hspace{1.5in} \text{as in}\,\, 2:1 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 3.6.1.

    Ratios are fractions used to compare two numbers.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.6.2.

    A car can drive 120 miles on 3 gallons of gas. Write down and simplify a ratio comparing the miles driven to the required gallons of gas (the car’s gas mileage).
    🔗
    Solution: The car’s gas mileage is the fraction:
    \begin{equation*} \frac{120\,\,\text{miles}}{3\,\,\text{gallons}} \end{equation*}
    This fraction can be simplified by separately considering the numbers and the units in the fraction.
    \begin{equation*} \frac{120\,\,\text{miles}}{3\,\,\text{gallons}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 40 \frac{\text{miles}}{\text{gallons}} \end{equation*}
    The car’s gas mileage is 40 miles / gallon, read aloud as “40 miles per gallon.”
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.6.3.

    A recipe making 36 cookies requires 2 eggs. Write down and simplify a ratio comparing the number of cookies to the number of required eggs.
    🔗
    Solution: The recipe’s ratio is
    \begin{equation*} \frac{36\,\,\text{cookies}}{2\,\,\text{eggs}} \end{equation*}
    Separate the numbers and the units and simplify each part separately:
    \begin{equation*} \frac{36\,\,\text{cookies}}{2\,\,\text{eggs}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 18 \frac{\text{cookies}}{\text{eggs}} \end{equation*}
    The ratio is 18 cookies / egg, read aloud as “18 cookies per egg.”
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.6.4.

    A person hikes 8 miles in 3 hours. Find the rate at which they’re walking.
    🔗
    Solution: The rate at which they’re walking is the ratio of distance traveled to time required.
    \begin{equation*} \frac{8\,\,\text{miles}}{3\,\,\text{hours}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{8}{3}\,\frac{text{\miles}}{\text{hours}} \end{equation*}
    The rate is 8/3 miles per hour.
    🔗
    🔗
    Special ratios can be written when you’re comparing two quantities that have the same units.
    🔗

    Example 3.6.5.

    Suppose a pizza dough recipe requires 110 grams of flour to be mixed with 200 grams of water. Write the ratio of flour to water for the recipe.
    🔗
    Solution: As a fraction, this ratio is
    \begin{equation*} \frac{200\,\,\text{grams flour}}{110\,\,\text{grams water}} \end{equation*}
    This can be simplified to
    \begin{equation*} \frac{200}{110} \,\frac{\text{grams flour}}{\text{grams water}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{200\div 10}{110\div 10} \,\frac{\text{grams flour}}{\text{grams water}} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \frac{20}{11}\,\,\frac{\text{grams flour}}{\text{grams water}} \end{equation*}
    But because both the flour and the water are being measured in the same units (grams), this ratio can be written using special notation:
    \begin{equation*} 20\,\,:\,\,11 \end{equation*}
    Read aloud, we would say “the ratio of flour to water is 20 to 11.”
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 3.6.6.

    Open Google Maps and look at the bottom right corner of the screen to find the “map scale.”
    🔗
    This map scale means the length of that white bar (about 2 inches) on your screen corresponds to 500 feet of distance in the real world.
    🔗
    Using this map scale, write the ratio of map distance to real world distance.
    🔗
    Solution: According to the map scale, the map distance is 2 inches. The corresponding real-world distance is 500 feet. Right now, the distance units aren’t the same. But we can convert all the distances to inches.
    🔗
    We look up:
    \begin{equation*} 12\,\,\text{inches} = 1\,\,\text{foot} \end{equation*}
    So we calculate:
    \begin{equation*} 500 \,\,\text{feet}\times \frac{12\,\,\text{inches}}{1\,\,\text{foot}} = 6000\,\,\text{inches} \end{equation*}
    Now, the map distance (2 inches) and the real-world distance (6000 inches) have the same units. So the ratio of map distance to real-world distance can be written:
    \begin{equation*} 2\,\,:\,\,6000 \end{equation*}
    Or, after being simplified:
    \begin{equation*} 1\,\,:\,\,3000 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    (a)
    There are 18 apples and 24 oranges in a basket. Write the ratio of apples to oranges and simplify it.

    🔗
    (b)
    A recipe uses 30 cups of flour and 18 cups of sugar. Write the ratio of flour to sugar and simplify.
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    A parking lot has 45 cars and 60 motorcycles. Write the ratio of cars to motorcycles in simplest form.
    🔗
    🔗
    (d)
    A cyclist rides 48 miles in 6 hours. Write the rate in miles per hour and simplify.
    🔗
    🔗
    (e)
    A machine produces 210 parts in 14 hours. Write the rate in parts per hour and simplify.
    🔗
    🔗
    (f)
    A runner runs 9 miles in 72 minutes. Write the rate in miles per minute and simplify.
    🔗
    🔗
    (g)
    You earn $150 for 12 hours of work. Write the rate in dollars per hour and simplify.
    🔗
    🔗
    (h)
    A printer uses 500 sheets of paper in 20 minutes. Write the rate in sheets per minute and simplify.

    Use unit conversion and write the following ratios:

    (a)
    A ribbon is 120 centimeters long and another ribbon is 3 meters long. Convert all the lengths to the same units, then write the ratio of the first ribbon to the second ribbon.

    🔗

    🔗

    A package weighs 900 grams, and another package weighs 2 kilograms. Convert all the weights to the same units, then write the ratio of the first trail’s length to the second trail’s length.
